### **IV. Operational Dynamics: The High-Resolution Strobe**

The Cholinergic System operates as a **State-Dependent Gain Controller**:

1. **Baseline (Theta):** In a low-arousal state, the system samples the environment broadly. Attention is diffuse.  
2. **Detection:** The **Salience Network** identifies a high-value stimulus (Novelty or Goal).  
3. **Surge:** A burst of **Acetylcholine (ACh)** is released from the Basal Forebrain.  
4. **Acceleration:** The attentional strobe "flickers" faster (Alpha/8Hz). The system takes more "frames" per second of the target.  
5. **Refinement:** Pyramidal neurons in the cortex become more sensitive to incoming sensory data and less sensitive to internal background noise.  
6. **Encoding:** The high ACh level triggers **$\\eta$ (Plasticity)**, ensuring the high-resolution data is "braided" into the **Palimpsest**.
